Our CEO & Founder

Anju K

(The Wisdom Pro)

Anju is a domestic violence and sexual assault survivor. During her 13-year relationship, she suffered and survived every form of abuse and violence. To count a few: 100+ head concussions and injuries, permanent eye damage, 20+ abortions (surgical/non-surgical) and extensive damage to her brain and nervous system.

She always says, “The best thing is, I am still alive, and the worst thing is, I was killed every night.”

She came to United States with her 2 kids and her abusive husband. As a result of an accident, they got stuck here and couldn’t go back. The violence and abuse in the home kept on increasing day by day till her daughter got hospitalized in critical condition due to the affects. This made Ms. Anju to open about the abuse in front of the doctors. When her daughter was released from the hospital, she was moved to a safe place, the Battered Women and Children’s Shelter. She calls it her re-birth as she was a resident there for 9 months.Her life has been a struggle during and after the abuse. As a result of continuous abuse & trauma, she can’t drive vehicles or work any regular job even though she is very well educated. Her mental/medical conditions and immigration status doesn’t allow her to start a new life, where she already has lost everything. During her recovery towards healing, she found her purpose - she turned her pain into her passion and started a journey to find her peace.

She started her blog Healing2Peace while still living in the shelter in search of a safe space for herself where she can uncover her sufferings without any judgements. She realized that journaling and documenting her journey, not only helped her and her medical team, but others could relate and found help for themselves too. This also connected her to other survivors, and she found that she is not alone.

She was journaling and documenting her life for her self-awareness, but never thought to become a writer. She didn’t believe in herself. Then one day, one more blessing came into her life, her family/editor friend, who encouraged her to start believing and write more, so she did.

Everyone says that she is blessed, but she says that she is grateful as it took a village to make her survival possible - the teamwork of individuals, non-profit organizations and community resources who opened their doors to her. She believes in appreciating the good work and acts of kindness.

She was never alone.

Since May 2021, she has met many strangers who came forward to embrace her family with kindness and compassion, which changed her perspective of life. She started going to therapy and counseling, which was her mental and emotional support throughout her journey. Finding the right therapist is very important, especially with the history of domestic violence. Working with the right therapist helped her to process and understand her trauma and increased the recovery speed.

Now in just a year, she fulfilled her necessities and got her health stable. Working in between her available odd hours (because of constant travel in public transport to her many doctor visits), she has filled her life with integrity and faithfulness. She is on a mission to make an impact on others by leaving a legacy of hope and Healing2Peace.
